# RESEARCH TRACK

## Collective Discoverability

(Multi-Observer Discovery)

STATUS: OPEN

ORIGIN:

Phase 16 established that Observation depends on the Observer.

VISUAL TEST 002 demonstrated operationally that:

Reachability ≠ Discoverability

and that Information affects Discoverability.

INSPIRATION:

Synthwave (Trac Network) demonstrates that multiple observers can contribute to a synthesized result.

This track investigates whether a similar principle exists at the level of Discoverability rather than inference quality.

---

## Research Boundary

This track does not question the validity of:

* Connectivity
* Reachability
* Discoverability

as currently defined in Phase 16.

Instead, it asks whether those concepts have meaningful collective counterparts when multiple observers are considered.

The purpose of this track is exploration, not revision of existing ontology.

---

## Research Question 1

### Collective Discoverability

Can multiple observers discover something that no individual observer can discover alone?

---

## Research Question 2

### Collective Critique

Can multiple observers identify errors that a single observer misses?

---

## Research Question 3

### Collective Selection

How are observations synthesized without creating a central authority?

---

## Research Question 4

### Sovereign Coordination

How do observers cooperate without surrendering sovereignty?

---

## Why This Matters To Freedeon

Phase 16 explored the relationship between:

Topology

and

Observation.

Synthwave suggests a new direction:

Observation

and

Collective Observation.

This research track explores whether Discoverability can emerge from the interaction of multiple observers.

No ontological claims are made at this stage.

No implementation is planned.

This is a research track, not a Phase.

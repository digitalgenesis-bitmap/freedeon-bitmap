# Freedeon MVP

STATUS: DRAFT

---

## Objective

Enable an external visitor to understand, in less than three minutes, that observed reality depends on the observer.

---

## The Visitor Journey

### Moment 1 — The World

Experience:

The visitor sees the complete graph.

No complex colors.

Only states and transitions.

Mental State:

"I understand the map."

"This is what exists."

Interaction:

The visitor freely explores the graph.

The experience feels stable and objective.

---

### Moment 2 — The Observer

Experience:

The visitor selects an Explorer Profile.

Examples:

* ALPHA
* BETA
* GAMMA

Action:

The graph changes instantly.

Mental State:

"Why did the graph change if the world stayed the same?"

Impact:

The visitor realizes that structure is fixed, but observation depends on the observer.

---

### Moment 3 — The Question

Experience:

The interface asks a single question:

"Why did node F change?"

Interaction:

The visitor clicks the node.

The explanation appears.

Examples:

"This observer lacked the required capability."

"This observer lacked the required information."

Mental State:

"The observed reality depends on the observer."

---

## Success Condition

The visitor leaves with an intuitive understanding that observation is a function of the observer.

The visitor does not need to understand:

* Ontology
* Phase 16
* Discoverability
* Reachability

The insight should be experiential.

---

## What Freedeon Is Not

Freedeon is not a graph viewer.

Freedeon is not a pathfinding tool.

Freedeon is not a topology explorer.

Those are mechanisms.

The purpose of the experience is to reveal that observation depends on the observer.

If a visitor leaves understanding the graph but not understanding the observer, the MVP has failed.

---

## Success Metric

A visitor with no prior knowledge of:

* Freedeon
* Phase 16
* Ontology
* Discoverability

should be able to answer the following question after using the MVP:

"Can two observers looking at the same world experience different realities?"

Expected answer:

"Yes, because they possess different knowledge and capabilities."

---

## Design Principles

### Neutrality First

Do not explain before the visitor asks.

Discovery should precede explanation.

### Interaction First

The visitor must actively choose the observer.

The insight should be experienced, not told.

### Minimal Text

The revelation should be primarily visual.

If a paragraph is required to understand the insight, the MVP has failed.

### Observer-Centric Design

Every design decision should be evaluated against one question:

"Does this help the visitor experience Moment 2?"
